Mind-Blowing Momentum Facts!
A bowling ball rolling down a lane|Did you know that a bowling ball's momentum is what makes it knock down pins?  The heavier and faster it moves, the greater its momentum.
A speeding car suddenly braking|Momentum is why it takes time to stop a fast-moving car.  The larger its mass and speed, the longer it takes to change its momentum.
A rocket launching into space|Rockets use momentum to propel themselves into space.  Burning fuel creates a force that increases their momentum, pushing them upwards.
A child on a swing|A child on a swing has momentum;  the higher the swing, the greater the momentum before it begins to fall.
A billiard ball hitting another|Momentum transfers from the cue ball to the target ball.  The momentum is conserved during the collision.
A hockey puck sliding on ice|Friction affects momentum – it gradually reduces the puck's momentum until it stops.
A pendulum swinging back and forth|A pendulum demonstrates conservation of momentum, continually trading kinetic and potential energy.
A wrecking ball demolishing a building|The massive momentum of a wrecking ball allows it to easily destroy structures.
A satellite orbiting Earth|A satellite in orbit has a constant momentum due to its speed and Earth's gravitational pull.
Two ice skaters colliding and moving together|When the two skaters collide, momentum is conserved. The final velocity depends on their initial momentum.
A person jumping from a trampoline|A person's jump on the trampoline involves an exchange of momentum between the person and the trampoline surface.
A train slowing down at a station|The brakes apply a force to the train, reducing its momentum until it comes to a complete stop.
