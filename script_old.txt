Amazing Animal Facts You Probably Didn't Know!
A close-up of a hummingbird's beak hovering near a flower |  Did you know that hummingbirds can fly backwards?  Their unique wing structure allows for this amazing feat.
A group of sloths hanging from a tree branch | Sloths are such slow movers that algae often grows on their fur, giving them a camouflage effect.
An elephant drinking water from a watering hole | Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth and can drink up to 50 gallons of water in a single sitting.
A brightly colored poison dart frog in a rainforest | Some poison dart frogs are so toxic that their venom can kill a human. The poison is often obtained through their diet.
A majestic lion pride resting in the African savanna |  Lionesses do most of the hunting for the pride, working together strategically to take down prey.
An octopus camouflaging itself on a coral reef | Octopuses are masters of disguise and can change their skin color and texture to blend in with their surroundings in a matter of seconds.
A seahorse holding onto a piece of seaweed | Male seahorses are the ones who carry and give birth to the eggs.
A giraffe stretching its neck to reach high leaves | Giraffes have the longest necks of any mammal and can reach up to 18 feet tall.
A polar bear hunting seals in the arctic | Polar bears have a thick layer of blubber that keeps them warm in freezing temperatures.  
A group of penguins huddled together in Antarctica | Emperor penguins can withstand temperatures as low as -60°C (-76°F) and endure months of harsh winter conditions.
A close-up of a chameleon's eye |  Chameleons can move each of their eyes independently, allowing them to see in two directions at once.
A stunning image of a colourful butterfly on a flower |  Butterflies taste with their feet.  They have taste receptors on their feet to find the best flowers for nectar.
